🍳 Camp Cooking Information

Meal Planning

You need to bring: Dinner for Saturday night and breakfast for Sunday morning

Eat lunch on Saturday before arriving

Picnic tables available for meal preparation and dining

Access to a two-burner camp stove for cooking

🔥 Camp Stove Information

  • Two-burner propane stoves provided for adults to use
  • Internal lighter ignition (similar to home stove)
  • Staff demonstration during Camp Cooking program
  • Adult supervision required for stove operation

📋 Kitchen Preparation Tips

  1. Plan meals: Decide what to eat for dinner and breakfast
  2. Shop ahead: Buy groceries in advance
  3. Pre-prep: Chop vegetables, wash fruit, cook meat the day before
  4. Store properly: Use plastic containers for prepared ingredients
  5. Pack cooler: Freeze ice packs and pack the morning of the event

🧊 Cooler Packing Guide

  • Layer method: Ice on bottom → perishables (meat/dairy) → more ice → more food → vegetables on top
  • Ice types: Reusable ice packs and large blocks last longer than small cubes
  • Meat safety: Keep at 5°C (41°F) or lower at all times

🧽 Clean-Up Process

  1. Boil water on the camp stove
  2. Divide warm water into two dish bins (soapy water + rinse water)
  3. Add biodegradable soap (a little goes a long way!)
  4. Scrape food scraps into compost bin first
  5. Wash dishes cleanest to dirtiest
  6. Rinse in second bin
  7. Dry with towel and put away
  8. Dispose of water 100+ feet from water sources

🐻 Food Storage Safety

NEVER keep food in your tent overnight! Store all food, cooking utensils, tablecloths, and scented items in your vehicle before bed to avoid attracting wildlife.
